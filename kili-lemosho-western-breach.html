Lemosho via Western Breach

Scenic Lemosho approach, then an advanced ascent through the Western Breach and Arrow Glacier to the crater.

8–9 days Challenging Helmet recommended
Western Breach

Why choose this variant

You get the best acclimatization and scenery of Lemosho, with the drama and directness of the Western Breach finish into the crater.

Big scenery Crater access

Fast facts

Start Lemosho Gate • Camps: Mti Mkubwa, Shira 1/2, Lava Tower, Arrow Glacier • Summit via Western Breach • Finish Mweka.

Advanced terrain Cold crater night (optional)

Sample 8-Day Itinerary

Optimized for acclimatization before the Western Breach ascent.

Day 1 Lemosho Gate → Mti Mkubwa
Forest
Easy start.

Enter lush forest to reach Mti Mkubwa (Big Tree) Camp.

Mti Mkubwa
Day 2 Mti Mkubwa → Shira 1
Moorland
Gain views.

Climb onto the Shira Plateau with expansive vistas.

Shira 1
Day 3 Shira 1 → Shira 2
Plateau traverse
Shorter day.

Continue across Shira to Shira 2, active acclimatization walk in afternoon.

Shira 2
Day 4 Shira 2 → Lava Tower → Arrow Glacier
Climb high
Sleep high.

Ascend past Lava Tower and on to Arrow Glacier Camp to stage for the Breach.

Arrow Glacier
Day 5 Western Breach → Crater
Technical ascent
Helmet advised.

Pre-dawn start up the Western Breach to enter the crater; optional crater camp visit (Furtwängler Glacier).

Crater
Day 6 Uhuru Peak → Mweka
Summit
Descend south.

Short push to Uhuru from the crater, then a long descent to Mweka Camp.

Uhuru
Day 7 Contingency/Rest
Buffer

Weather or acclimatization buffer day if needed.

Buffer

Exit
Celebrate.
