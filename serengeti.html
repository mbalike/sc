Destination

Serengeti National Park

Endless savannahs, thundering hooves, and predators on the prowl — the Serengeti is the stage for Earth’s greatest wildlife drama.

Discover Serengeti View Safari Itineraries

Overview

The Serengeti spans 30,000 km² of pristine wilderness. Each year, over two million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles circle the ecosystem following the rains — the legendary Great Migration.

Big Five Predator action Balloon safaris

Top Activities

  • Dawn and dusk game drives with expert guides
  • Sunrise hot air balloon flight with champagne bush breakfast
  • Photo safaris focused on big cats and migration crossings
  • Visit Olduvai Gorge Museum (en route to Ngorongoro)

Where To Stay

Choose from mobile tented camps that follow the herds or luxe permanent lodges with pools and spas. We’ll match your season and style.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Northern Tanzania
  • Famous for: Great Migration, big cats
  • Best for: Wildlife lovers, photographers
  • Nearest Hub: Arusha / Kilimanjaro Airport

Why Serengeti?

A spectacle like no other.

The Great Migration

Witness over two million wildebeest and zebra moving across the plains — one of the most powerful wildlife events on Earth.

Big Cat Territory

Home to some of Africa’s highest concentrations of lions, cheetahs, and leopards — raw, real predator action.

Endless Plains

Iconic golden savannahs that stretch beyond the horizon — the true definition of untamed Africa.

Balloon Safaris

Float above the Serengeti at sunrise, followed by a champagne bush breakfast — safari, elevated.

Year-Round Game Viewing

No matter the season, the Serengeti delivers — from river crossings to calving season drama.

Legendary Landscapes

From kopjes to open plains, every corner feels cinematic and unforgettable.

Insider Tips

  • Crossings are unpredictable: 2–3 nights in the north (Jul–Oct) increase your chances.
  • Calving season (Jan–Mar) delivers intense predator action and incredible photographic moments.
  • For the best shots, bring a bean bag or monopod for steady long-lens captures.
