Ngorongoro Crater

A lost world of meadows, forests, lakes and marsh — protected by towering crater walls and home to an astounding density of wildlife.

Explore Guide See Trips

Overview

The Crater floor spans ~260 km², with grasslands, Lerai forest, and the alkaline Lake Magadi. Its natural enclosure creates a wildlife haven — great for short, high-impact game drives.

High wildlife density Epic vistas UNESCO biosphere

Top Activities

  • Crater descent for morning/afternoon game drives
  • Rim viewpoints for sweeping panoramas
  • Cultural visits with nearby Maasai communities

Where To Stay

Lodges on the rim offer incredible sunrise views; crater floor access is regulated to preserve the ecosystem.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Northern Tanzania
  • Type: Volcanic caldera
  • Best for: Short, high-yield drives
  • Nearest Hub: Arusha
