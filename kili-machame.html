Kilimanjaro

Machame Route

The popular “Whiskey” route following the Southern Circuit with the iconic Barranco Wall. A balanced mix of scenery, challenge, and acclimatization over 6–7 days.

6–7 days Moderate–Challenging Barranco Wall views
Machame

Why Choose Machame

Diverse landscapes from rainforest to alpine desert, a well-paced ascent, and the thrilling Barranco Wall make Machame a favorite for trekkers seeking classic Kilimanjaro drama.

Rainforest to high desert Southern Circuit Night summit

Fast Facts

Start: Machame Gate • End: Mweka Gate • Overnights: tents • Good acclimatization with Lava Tower “walk high, sleep low.”

Shira • Barranco • Karanga • Barafu Gradual gain

Sample 7-Day Itinerary

Also offered as 6 days (faster). We recommend 7 days for better acclimatization.

Day 1 Machame Gate → Machame Camp
Forest ascent
Steady climb through montane forest.

Registration at the gate, trek to Machame Camp with chances to spot colobus monkeys.

Machame
Day 2 Machame → Shira Camp
Onto Shira
Steeper sections into heath/moorland.

Ascend above the trees to the Shira Plateau with grand views of Kibo.

Shira
Day 3 Shira → Lava Tower → Barranco
Acclimatize
Walk high, sleep low.

Climb to ~4,600 m at Lava Tower, descend to the lush Barranco Valley.

Lava Tower
Day 4 Barranco → Karanga
Barranco Wall
A signature moment — dramatic, scenic, unforgettable.

Short and spectacular day up the Barranco Wall, then traverse valleys to Karanga.

Barranco
Day 5 Karanga → Barafu
High camp
Early dinner and rest.

Arrive Barafu (~4,673 m) and prepare gear for summit night.

Barafu
Day 6 Summit → Mweka
Uhuru Peak
Midnight start to the Roof of Africa.

Ascend via Stella Point to Uhuru (5,895 m), then descend to Mweka Camp.

Summit
Day 7 Mweka → Gate
Final Descent
Mweka Route.

Follow the trail down to Mweka Gate, where you'll complete the climb, receive your certificates, and transfer onward.

Mweka
