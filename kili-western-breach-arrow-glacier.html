Kilimanjaro

Western Breach (Arrow Glacier)

The most direct and advanced route into Kilimanjaro's crater via Arrow Glacier—steep, exposed, and spectacularly rewarding.

6-day add-on Very challenging Helmet recommended
Arrow Glacier

Who Is It For?

Experienced trekkers confident on steep, sometimes loose terrain and comfortable with early alpine starts. Highly recommended for climbers with strong acclimatization and a focus on safety.

Pre-acclimatized Safety focus

Fast Facts

  • Stage at Arrow Glacier after Machame or Lemosho.
  • Enter the crater via the dramatic Western Breach.
  • Optional crater camp for glacier and ash pit exploration.
  • Summit via Uhuru, descend through the lush Mweka route.
  • Best suited for acclimatized, technically confident hikers.

6-Day Sample Add-On Flow

This outlines the summit phase once acclimatized via Machame or Lemosho.

Day 1 Lava Tower → Arrow Glacier
High Camp Positioning
Final gear readiness.

Move up to Arrow Glacier for an early dinner and final equipment prep ahead of the ascent.

Arrow Glacier
Day 2 Western Breach → Crater
Technical Breach Ascent
Protective gear recommended.

Begin a pre-dawn climb through the Western Breach, entering the crater as the light rises. Explore the ash pit or Furtwängler Glacier if conditions allow.

Crater
Day 3 Uhuru Peak → Mweka
Summit Push
Scenic southern descent.

A short but powerful push to Uhuru Peak before beginning the long, rewarding descent toward Mweka.

Uhuru
Day 4 Mweka → Gate
Final Descent
Mweka Route.

Complete your descent through the lush forest and arrive at Mweka Gate to receive certificates and transfer onward.

Mweka Gate
